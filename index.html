How it works
Four steps between
impulse and regret.
01
Intercept
The moment you click Add to Cart or Buy Now on any major shopping site, pause. catches the request before it fires.
02
10s Timer
A full-screen overlay appears with a 10-second countdown. The product name and exact price are pulled directly from the cart payload.
03
Redirect
Choose to save the money instead. The would-be spend is logged toward your savings goal. Buy anyway if it was planned — no judgment.
04
Compound
Your dashboard tracks every paused purchase, total saved, and projects the long-term impact of your decisions. Small pauses, big numbers.

The philosophy
Buying things is easy.
Wanting things is the problem.

Every major ecommerce platform has spent billions of dollars engineering away the moment between wanting something and owning it. One-click checkout. Saved payment methods. Countdown timers. They are designed to make you act before you think.

pause. puts that moment back. Not to stop you from buying — but to make sure the decision is actually yours.

🧠
Behavioral science
Present bias makes us overvalue now at the expense of later. A 10-second pause is enough to break the automatic response.
🔒
No judgment
If you decide to buy, you buy. pause. isn't a blocker — it's a mirror. The choice is always yours.
🌱
Small acts, big change
Skipping one $50 impulse buy per week is $2,600 a year. pause. shows you the math in real time.
